Positive Cycle Arbitrage Graph

This graph shows each cryptocurrency at a number of exchanges as vertices. The graph's edges can be thought of as the exchange rates between these currencies. We modified the Bellman Ford algorithm to look for positive cycles in the graph, and these positive cycles are seen as the vertices and edges in the center of the graph. The green arrows between vertices in the center of the graph show a path of currency trading which, if followed, will allow one to end up with more of the same currency they started out with. This process, known as arbitrage, makes use of slight fluctuations in cryptocurrency exchange rates across different exchanges in order to gain currency solely through trading at the right times.

Cryptocurrency Prices Over Time

The graph at left shows the average prices of cryptocurrencies in the recent past. The fluctuations in these cryptocurrency prices are part of what allow our project to work. Although a currency might be generally moving up or down, these movements take slighty different amounts of time on different exchanges.
